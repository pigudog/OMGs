- Selection between PARP inhibitors and anti-VEGF therapy at relapse should consider histotype, BRCA1/2 status, prior PARPi/bevacizumab exposure, and cumulative platinum exposure, as these factors influence benefit and toxicity in recurrent epithelial ovarian cancer. [@guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10]

- For first relapse occurring >6 months after last platinum, referral to an experienced gynecologic oncology center for evaluation of secondary cytoreduction is recommended, with decisions guided by likelihood of complete resection, residual toxicity, performance status, and patient preference. [@guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|11]

- NCCN emphasizes that “platinum-sensitive” vs “platinum-resistant” status is a spectrum; clinicians should apply judgment and flexibility when selecting systemic therapy, especially where data are limited (e.g., low-grade or clear cell histologies), and should incorporate maintenance strategies and surveillance accordingly. [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|18]

- NCCN notes limited data for primary and maintenance therapy in recurrent/persistent low-grade or clear cell ovarian cancers and recommends individualized treatment planning, including consideration of systemic therapy options, clinical trials, and close monitoring during and after treatment. [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14]

- For first recurrence, platinum-based chemotherapy is the preferred option (category 1) when appropriate, with consideration of secondary cytoreductive surgery and/or best supportive care; immediate platinum-based recurrence therapy may also be used as a category 2B option depending on clinical context. [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|15]

- A case report in platinum-refractory metastatic endometrial clear cell carcinoma showed a durable response to pembrolizumab combined with localized radiotherapy, suggesting potential benefit of immunotherapy plus radiation (abscopal effect) in aggressive clear cell histologies when standard chemotherapy fails. [@pubmed:36591227]

- Ovarian clear cell carcinoma is characterized by association with endometriosis, frequent ARID1A and PIK3CA alterations, chemoresistance to platinum-based regimens, and may benefit from strategies targeting angiogenesis, PI3K/AKT/mTOR, DNA damage response, or immune pathways; enrollment in clinical trials is encouraged. [@pubmed:33758607]

- A case report of recurrent ovarian clear cell carcinoma with ARID1A mutation demonstrated a remarkable response to third-line triplet therapy (olaparib + pembrolizumab + bevacizumab), supporting consideration of PARP inhibitor–immunotherapy–anti-VEGF combinations in selected, heavily pretreated clear cell cases, ideally within trials. [@pubmed:35401005]

- A novel ovarian clear cell carcinoma cell line (NUCOLL43) with extensive genomic loss of heterozygosity and cisplatin sensitivity in vitro highlights potential responsiveness to DNA-damaging agents and supports further exploration of platinum and DNA repair–targeted strategies in this histology. [@pubmed:30109783]

- Case series of ovarian clear cell carcinoma arising from endometriosis underscore the malignant potential of endometriosis, the often aggressive course of clear cell tumors, and the need for vigilant follow-up and timely intervention in endometriosis-associated ovarian masses. [@pubmed:37901720]
