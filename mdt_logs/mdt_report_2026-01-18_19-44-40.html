- Perform validated molecular testing on most recent tumor tissue (in CLIA-approved lab) to at minimum assess BRCA1/2 and broader homologous recombination deficiency (HRD) status, as these results guide use of PARP inhibitors and other targeted strategies in high‑grade serous/endometrioid ovarian cancer. - Use platinum-sensitivity as a spectrum rather than a strict cutoff when planning recurrent disease therapy, applying clinical judgment to select and sequence systemic options and considering maintenance approaches accordingly. [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14]

- In platinum-sensitive relapsed ovarian cancer, offer PARP inhibitor maintenance (e.g., olaparib) after response to platinum-based chemotherapy, with strongest benefit in BRCA‑mutated/HRD‑positive disease but clinically meaningful benefit also observed more broadly. [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|11][@pubmed:31205507]

- For patients with epithelial versus non-epithelial ovarian neoplasms, stage and histologic subtype should determine eligibility for in vivo ovarian tissue preservation as part of fertility-sparing management, following histology- and stage-specific indications.
