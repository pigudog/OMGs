Digest (<=8 bullets)

- In early-stage epithelial ovarian cancer, high‑grade endometrioid carcinoma has prognosis similar to high‑grade serous; adjuvant platinum‑based chemotherapy improves survival overall, but a clear survival benefit for adjuvant chemotherapy in stage IA/IB low‑grade endometrioid or clear‑cell carcinoma has not been demonstrated and should be individualized [esgo_esmo_esp_consensus_ovariancancer_2024__v00000000].  
- For recurrent ovarian cancer, “platinum‑sensitive” vs “platinum‑resistant” represents a continuum; NCCN recommends using clinical judgment and flexibility (prior therapies, interval from last platinum, toxicity, patient status) when deciding systemic therapy and trial options [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000].  
- At first recurrence after platinum‑sensitive disease, platinum‑based combination chemotherapy is preferred (category 1) and may be combined with maintenance strategies; consider secondary cytoreductive surgery in appropriately selected patients and always integrate best supportive care as needed [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000].  
- In relapsed disease, selection between PARP inhibitors and anti‑VEGF therapy should account for histologic subtype, BRCA1/2 status, prior PARP/anti‑VEGF exposure, expected toxicities, treatment goals, and patient preference; cumulative prior platinum exposure influences benefit and toxicity profiles [esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000].  
- Data on primary and maintenance therapy for recurrent/persistent low‑grade or clear‑cell ovarian carcinoma are limited; individualized treatment decisions and clinical trial participation are encouraged [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000].  
- For malignant ovarian germ‑cell tumors (e.g., immature teratoma, dysgerminoma, yolk‑sac tumors) and select borderline tumors, fertility‑sparing surgery with ovarian preservation is generally acceptable when complete staging and appropriate adjuvant therapy are provided; prognosis is often favorable, supporting conservative approaches in suitable candidates [esgo_eshre_esge_fertilitysparing_pocket_2025__v00000000].  
- Ovarian preservation decisions must consider tumor type, stage, and prognostic factors; in borderline tumors with non‑invasive peritoneal implants, fertility‑sparing options are possible but require careful counseling on recurrence risk and long‑term surveillance [esgo_eshre_esge_fertilitysparing_pocket_2025__v00000000].
