Digest (<=8 bullets)

- NCCN 2025: For apparent stage I epithelial ovarian cancer, adjuvant platinum-based chemotherapy is recommended for grade 3 endometrioid and high-grade serous carcinomas; observation may be considered for lower-risk (eg, grade 1–2) endometrioid histology after comprehensive staging [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000 p11, p15].  
- NCCN 2025: Primary systemic regimens for stage II–IV epithelial ovarian cancer are carboplatin plus paclitaxel (IV q3wk) or other platinum-taxane combinations; these regimens are also used as adjuvant therapy in higher-risk early-stage disease [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000 p15].  
- ESGO/ESMO/ESP 2024: High‑grade endometrioid ovarian carcinoma has prognosis similar to high‑grade serous carcinoma and clearly worse than low‑grade endometrioid, supporting use of adjuvant platinum-based chemotherapy for high‑grade disease even in early stages [esgo_esmo_esp_consensus_ovariancancer_2024__v00000000 p6].  
- ESGO/ESMO/ESP 2024: Evidence for survival benefit of adjuvant chemotherapy in stage I endometrioid carcinoma is uncertain overall, but high‑risk features (high grade, incomplete staging, or other adverse factors) favor recommending adjuvant platinum-based chemotherapy [esgo_esmo_esp_consensus_ovariancancer_2024__v00000000 p6].  
- NCCN 2025: Imaging for staging or restaging should include contrast-enhanced CT or MRI of chest/abdomen/pelvis; PET/CT can be used as clinically indicated to clarify equivocal findings or suspected metastases [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000 p11, p22].  
- NCCN 2025: In patients with contraindications to iodinated contrast or significant renal dysfunction, MRI with gadolinium (if not contraindicated) is preferred over CT; chest CT may be done with or without IV contrast depending on renal/allergy status [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000 p22].  
- NCCN 2025: Post-treatment surveillance should use symptom review, physical examination, and CA‑125 (if initially elevated) at defined intervals; routine imaging is reserved for concerning symptoms, exam, or marker rise rather than done indiscriminately [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000 p11, p15].
