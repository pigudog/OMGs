Digest (<=8 bullets)

- In relapsed disease, selection between PARP inhibitors and anti-VEGF (bevacizumab) should consider histologic subtype, BRCA1/2 mutation status, prior PARPi and/or bevacizumab exposure, and cumulative platinum exposure, as these factors influence benefit and toxicity profiles [esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000].  

- Increasing cumulative exposure to platinum is associated with changing patterns of response and toxicity over the disease course; this should be factored into decisions about further platinum-based regimens and maintenance choices at relapse [esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000].  

- For first recurrence, NCCN recommends platinum-based chemotherapy as the preferred regimen (category 1) when appropriate, with consideration of secondary cytoreductive surgery and best supportive care options based on patient fitness and disease distribution [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000].  

- At recurrence, immediate platinum-based therapy can be considered (category 2B) as an alternative strategy, or best supportive care, depending on symptoms, prior treatments, and patient goals of care [nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000].  

- After response (CR/PR/NED) to platinum-based chemotherapy in relapse, maintenance with PARP inhibitors (niraparib or rucaparib) is recommended; olaparib alone or with bevacizumab and rucaparib are also endorsed as maintenance options in appropriate patients [esgo_esmo_esp_consensus_ovariancancer_2024__v00000000].  

- Bevacizumab used with chemotherapy and as maintenance in phase III trials was continued until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity; it should not be stopped solely for rising CA-125 in the absence of clinical or radiologic progression [esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000].  

- Maintenance choice (PARP inhibitor alone, bevacizumab alone, or combination) should be individualized based on molecular profile (e.g., BRCA/HRD), previous exposure to PARP or bevacizumab, and tolerance, as reflected in consensus guideline sections on who should receive bevacizumab or PARPi and for how long [esgo_ovariancancer_pocketguidelines_2025__v00000000].
