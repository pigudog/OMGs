Digest (<=8 bullets)

- In recurrent epithelial ovarian cancer, selection between PARP inhibitors and anti-VEGF therapy at relapse should consider histologic subtype, BRCA1/2 mutation status, prior PARP or bevacizumab exposure, and cumulative platinum exposure, as these factors influence benefit and toxicity from further platinum and targeted agents [@guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10].

- Definitions of platinum-sensitive versus platinum-resistant ovarian cancer represent a continuum; NCCN recommends using clinical judgment and flexibility when determining systemic therapy choices and maintenance strategies in recurrent disease, particularly in less common histologies such as low-grade or clear cell tumors where data are limited [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14].

- ESGO/ESHRE/ESGE guidance on fertility preservation stratifies the safety of in vivo ovarian tissue preservation by epithelial versus non-epithelial histology and by FIGO stage (IA–IC2), emphasizing that fertility-sparing approaches are more restricted in high-grade or clear cell epithelial cancers compared with borderline or non-epithelial tumors [@guideline:esgo_eshre_esge_fertilitysparing_pocket_2025__v00000000|17].

- For completely resected stage I–III ovarian dysgerminoma, carboplatin–etoposide has shown high activity with acceptable toxicity in phase II data and is considered a reasonable adjuvant alternative to BEP where toxicity concerns or comorbidities make BEP less suitable [@guideline:esmo_cpg_nonepithelialovarian_2018__v00000000|13].

- At first or subsequent recurrence of epithelial ovarian cancer, NCCN recommends considering secondary cytoreductive surgery in appropriate candidates and using platinum-based chemotherapy as preferred for platinum-sensitive relapse (category 1), while also integrating best supportive care discussions according to patient status and preferences [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|15].

- A case of platinum-refractory metastatic endometrial clear cell carcinoma achieved a durable response to pembrolizumab combined with localized radiotherapy, suggesting that immune checkpoint inhibition plus radiation (potential abscopal effect) may be an option in highly selected, heavily pretreated clear cell endometrial cancers lacking standard effective therapies [@pubmed:36591227].

- A reported ovarian clear cell carcinoma with ARID1A mutation showed a remarkable response to third-line triplet therapy with olaparib, pembrolizumab, and bevacizumab, supporting consideration of PARP inhibitor plus immunotherapy and anti-VEGF combinations in molecularly selected, heavily pretreated clear cell ovarian cancer when standard regimens are exhausted [@pubmed:35401005].

- In a single-institution series of 45 patients undergoing secondary debulking for recurrent ovarian clear cell carcinoma, surgical cytoreduction at relapse was feasible and associated with defined survival outcomes, supporting consideration of secondary debulking in carefully selected OCCC patients with resectable disease [@pubmed:32104073].

- Contemporary reviews highlight that ovarian clear cell carcinoma is biologically distinct, frequently associated with endometriosis, and often exhibits chemoresistance; this underpins the need to prioritize complete initial cytoreduction, consider clinical trials and targeted/immunotherapy strategies, and account for unique molecular features (e.g., ARID1A alterations) in treatment planning [@pubmed:33758607].

- Case series of ovarian clear cell carcinoma arising in the context of endometriosis emphasize that endometriosis-associated ovarian cancer, though rare, warrants vigilant long-term follow-up and timely imaging/surgical evaluation of suspicious endometriotic lesions, given the aggressive behavior of clear cell histology once malignant transformation occurs [@pubmed:37901720].
