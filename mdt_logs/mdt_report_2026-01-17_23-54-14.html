Digest (<=8 bullets)

- For first recurrence of epithelial ovarian cancer, platinum-based chemotherapy is preferred when appropriate; this recommendation is category 1 for platinum-sensitive relapse [4].  
- Secondary cytoreductive surgery should be considered at recurrence, with decision based on disease distribution, performance status, and likelihood of achieving complete resection [4].  
- If bevacizumab was used as part of primary therapy and the tumor is BRCA1/2 wild-type or unknown but homologous recombination–deficient (HRD), olaparib maintenance can be added; this is a category 2B recommendation [5].  
- NCCN systemic therapy options for recurrence are consolidated on the systemic therapy pages (OV-C 8 and OV-C 9), and should be used to select specific regimens based on platinum sensitivity and prior treatments [5].  
- For patients not suitable for further active oncologic treatment or declining therapy, best supportive care/palliative care should be integrated per NCCN Palliative Care guidelines [4].  
- The NCCN ovarian cancer recommendations cited are category 2A unless otherwise indicated, implying uniform consensus based on lower-level evidence; category 1 where specifically noted reflects high-level evidence with uniform consensus [1][2][3][5].
