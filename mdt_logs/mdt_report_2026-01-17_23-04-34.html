Digest (<=8 bullets)

- FIGO stage IIIC ovarian cancer includes macroscopic peritoneal metastases >2 cm beyond the pelvis and/or regional lymph node metastases without distant metastasis [1].  
- Platinum sensitivity in recurrent ovarian cancer is a spectrum; treatment choice (including platinum rechallenge vs non-platinum) should use clinical judgment rather than a rigid time cutoff [3].  
- For first recurrence of platinum-sensitive epithelial ovarian cancer, platinum-based combination chemotherapy is preferred and category 1 when appropriate [4].  
- In selected patients with recurrent disease, secondary cytoreductive surgery can be considered before systemic therapy, based on performance status, disease distribution, and likelihood of complete resection [4].  
- For recurrent disease where systemic therapy is not appropriate or not desired, best supportive care and palliative care pathways should be actively considered [3][4].  
- For malignant ovarian germ cell tumors with complete clinical response after initial therapy, surveillance is appropriate; if residual malignancy is present, additional chemotherapy such as TIP (paclitaxel/ifosfamide/cisplatin) or high-dose chemotherapy with hematopoietic cell transplant should be considered, ideally in a tertiary center [5].  
- Fertility-sparing approaches may be considered in selected non-epithelial ovarian tumors (e.g., dysgerminoma, yolk sac tumor, immature teratoma) with careful counseling about prognosis and adjuvant treatment implications [2].  
- Non-invasive peritoneal implants in selected ovarian tumors are associated with a more favorable prognosis than invasive implants and may influence decisions on the extent of surgery and adjuvant therapy [2].
