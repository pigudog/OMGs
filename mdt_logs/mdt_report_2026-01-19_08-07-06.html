Digest (<=8 bullets)

- NCCN recommends, for recurrent disease in survivorship, considering hormonal therapy or systemic chemotherapy (per OV-C), with treatment selection guided by platinum sensitivity (platinum-sensitive vs platinum-resistant) and allowing observation as an option in appropriate cases [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|22].  
- NCCN advises validated molecular testing on the most recent available tumor tissue in a CLIA-approved lab, at minimum to identify potentially actionable alterations, to inform systemic therapy and management of drug reactions in ovarian cancer [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|15].  
- ESMO recommends that, in relapse, choice between PARP inhibitors and anti-VEGF therapy should consider histotype, BRCA1/2 mutation status, prior exposure to PARP inhibitors and/or bevacizumab, and cumulative prior platinum, which can affect efficacy and tolerability of re‑treatment [@guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10].  
- NCCN lists platinum-based chemotherapy as preferred for first recurrence (category 1) in appropriate candidates, with consideration of secondary cytoreductive surgery and best supportive care; immediate platinum-based therapy at recurrence is category 2B and should be individualized [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|15].  
- NCCN notes that definitions of platinum-sensitive versus platinum-resistant disease exist on a continuum and should not be applied rigidly; clinical judgment is emphasized, and data for primary and maintenance therapy in recurrent/persistent low-grade clear cell or low-grade histologies are limited, warranting individualized treatment plans [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14].  
- Case report evidence suggests that a triplet regimen of olaparib, pembrolizumab, and bevacizumab can induce a remarkable response in third-line treatment of ARID1A-mutated ovarian clear cell carcinoma, supporting consideration of PARP inhibitor–based combinations and immunotherapy/anti-VEGF strategies in selected molecular subsets when standard options are exhausted [@pubmed:35401005].  
- Reviews of ovarian clear cell carcinoma highlight its distinct biology, frequent association with endometriosis, chemoresistance, and lack of standard therapy in recurrence; they support comprehensive molecular profiling (e.g., ARID1A and other targets) and consideration of clinical trial enrollment and non–platinum-based strategies in resistant disease [@pubmed:33758607].  
- A case report of a BRCA2-mutated secondary ovarian tumor originating from liver cancer describes benefit from olaparib, suggesting PARP inhibitors may be considered in BRCA-mutated metastatic adenocarcinomas involving the ovary, even when the primary is extra-ovarian, in the absence of standard options [@pubmed:36578942].  
- Case evidence in clear cell carcinoma of the endometrium shows durable response to pembrolizumab combined with localized radiotherapy, with a possible abscopal effect, supporting the MDT consideration of checkpoint inhibitors plus radiation in selected platinum-refractory clear cell gynecologic cancers, particularly within clinical trials or compassionate-use settings [@pubmed:36591227].  
- Preclinical characterization of the NUCOLL43 ovarian clear cell carcinoma cell line demonstrates high genomic instability and distinct drug sensitivity profiles, underscoring that OCCC may respond differently to cytotoxics and targeted agents than high-grade serous carcinoma, and supporting trial designs that evaluate OCCC-specific therapeutic strategies [@pubmed:30109783].
