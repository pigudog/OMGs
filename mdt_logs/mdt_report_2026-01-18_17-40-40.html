- Definitions of platinum-sensitive vs platinum-resistant relapse are a spectrum; clinical judgment and flexibility are recommended when choosing recurrence treatment and maintenance strategies, including PARPi or bevacizumab use [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14].

- In platinum-sensitive recurrent epithelial ovarian cancer, treatment is generally guided by platinum sensitivity status, with secondary cytoreductive surgery plus platinum-based chemotherapy considered in appropriately selected patients (good performance status, resectable disease) to improve outcomes [@pubmed:33850907; @pubmed:36338689].

- For platinum-sensitive relapse after response to platinum-based chemotherapy, PARP inhibitor maintenance (olaparib, niraparib, rucaparib) is recommended in patients in CR/PR/NED, with regimens chosen according to prior bevacizumab, BRCA/HRD status, and toxicity profile [@guideline:esgo_esmo_esp_consensus_ovariancancer_2024__v00000000|10; @guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10].

- Olaparib maintenance in platinum-sensitive relapsed ovarian cancer significantly prolongs progression-free survival, particularly in BRCA-mutated or HRD tumors, and is now standard of care in this setting after response to platinum [@pubmed:31205507; @pubmed:38525421].

- HRD and BRCA status should be reassessed or confirmed at relapse if not previously characterized, as they critically inform the choice and expected benefit of PARP inhibitor maintenance and other targeted strategies [@pubmed:38525421; @guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10].

- When considering PARPi vs bevacizumab at relapse, decision factors include histologic subtype, BRCA/HRD status, prior exposure to PARPi and/or bevacizumab, cumulative platinum exposure, expected toxicities, comorbidities, and patient preference [@guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10].

- For patients with platinum-resistant disease or those unsuitable for further platinum, non-platinum single-agent chemotherapy options include weekly paclitaxel, pegylated liposomal doxorubicin, topotecan, and gemcitabine; early integration of palliative care and prioritization for clinical trials is advised in fit patients [@guideline:esmo_cpg_epithelialovarian_2023__v00000000|10].

- Data on primary and maintenance therapy for less common histologies (e.g., low-grade or rare subtypes such as carcinosarcoma) are limited; management generally follows high-grade serous ovarian cancer paradigms, but participation in clinical trials or individualized off-label use (e.g., PARPi in selected cases) may be considered with multidisciplinary discussion [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14; @pubmed:34938651].
