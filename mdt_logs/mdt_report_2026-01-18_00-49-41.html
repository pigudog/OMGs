Digest (<=8 bullets)

- Carboplatin–etoposide is an active adjuvant option with acceptable toxicity for completely resected stage I–III dysgerminoma, based on a GOG phase II trial; however, it has not been directly compared with BEP in randomized studies [1].  
- Fertility-sparing surgery (with ovarian preservation) is an established option in selected malignant ovarian germ cell tumours, including dysgerminoma, yolk sac tumour, and immature teratoma, provided oncologic safety is ensured and risk factors are considered [2].  
- In advanced ovarian carcinoma, initial staging and treatment planning can use contrast-enhanced CT, MRI, or PET-CT, and expert transvaginal ultrasonography; reports should be structured to aid MDT decisions [3].  
- In recurrent epithelial ovarian cancer, choice between PARP inhibitors and anti-VEGF therapy at relapse should consider histologic subtype, BRCA1/2 mutation status, prior exposure to PARPi and/or bevacizumab, and expected toxicities [5].  
- Cumulative platinum exposure and prior lines of therapy influence both efficacy and toxicity in subsequent platinum-based treatments for relapsed epithelial ovarian cancer, and should guide regimen selection [5].  
- For low-grade serous ovarian carcinoma, the MEK inhibitor trametinib can be considered after prior platinum-based chemotherapy and hormone therapy, though it is not EMA approved and should be weighed against alternatives and access issues [4].  
- In relapsed epithelial ovarian cancer, long-term surveillance should balance the low likelihood of cure with the availability of effective therapies, integrating symptom assessment, imaging, and tumour markers to guide timely intervention [4].
