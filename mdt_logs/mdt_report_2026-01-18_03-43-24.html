Digest (<=8 bullets)

- For first recurrence after prior platinum-based chemotherapy, immediate platinum-based combination therapy is preferred when feasible; secondary cytoreductive surgery can be considered in selected patients, and best supportive care remains an option alongside or instead of systemic therapy [NCCN_ovariancancer_v3_2025 p15].  
- Treatment of recurrent disease should be individualized along a spectrum of platinum sensitivity vs resistance; rigid cut-offs are discouraged and clinical judgment is required when selecting systemic therapy and sequencing options [NCCN_ovariancancer_v3_2025 p14].  
- For low-grade or less common ovarian cancer histologies (e.g. LCOC), evidence for both primary and maintenance systemic therapy in the recurrent/persistent setting is limited, so extrapolation from high-grade serous data should be cautious and preferably within expert centers or trials [NCCN_ovariancancer_v3_2025 p14].  
- Validated molecular testing on the most recent available tumor tissue is recommended at recurrence, including at minimum assays to identify potential therapeutic targets (e.g. BRCA1/2 and other HRR genes, MSI/MMR, and other actionable alterations) to guide systemic and maintenance strategies [NCCN_ovariancancer_v3_2025 p15].  
- Adjuvant platinum-based chemotherapy significantly improves overall and progression-free survival in early-stage epithelial ovarian cancer (FIGO I–II), supporting platinum use as foundational systemic therapy and influencing subsequent relapse management strategies [ESMO_CPG_epithelialOvarian_2023 p4].  
- In the relapse setting, choice between PARP inhibitors and anti-VEGF therapies should integrate histologic subtype, BRCA1/2 mutation/HRD status, prior exposure to PARP inhibitors and/or bevacizumab, and cumulative platinum exposure, as these factors influence expected benefit and toxicity [ESMO_CPG_epithelialOvarian_2023 p10].  
- Reduced efficacy of PARP inhibitors is observed when used after extensive prior platinum and/or prior PARP exposure, so earlier-line use in appropriately selected patients is generally favored over reserving PARP for late relapse [ESMO_CPG_epithelialOvarian_2023 p10].  
- During and after treatment for recurrent disease, ongoing assessment of symptom burden, performance status, and patient preference is essential to decide between further active systemic therapy, clinical trial participation, or transition to best supportive care [NCCN_ovariancancer_v3_2025 p14–15].
