- Adjuvant platinum-based chemotherapy (typically carboplatin plus paclitaxel) is standard for most stage II–IV epithelial ovarian cancers and for selected high‑risk stage I disease based on histology and grade [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|14].  
- NCCN recommends universal germline BRCA1/2 testing and somatic tumor HRD/BRCA testing in epithelial ovarian cancer, as these results direct use of PARP inhibitor maintenance and other targeted strategies [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|18; @pubmed:38525421].  
- PARP inhibitor maintenance (alone or combined with bevacizumab, depending on prior therapy and biomarker status) is recommended after response to first‑line platinum-based chemotherapy in BRCA‑mutated and/or HRD‑positive high‑grade serous or endometrioid ovarian cancers [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|18; @pubmed:38525421].  
- For fertility preservation, ESGO/ESHRE/ESGE guidelines outline indications for in vivo ovarian tissue preservation and fertility‑sparing surgery based on histologic subtype and FIGO stage; these approaches are generally restricted to early-stage, low‑risk tumors and require specialized counseling [@guideline:esgo_eshre_esge_fertilitysparing_pocket_2025__v00000000|17].  
- NCCN emphasizes that treatment choice should account for histologic subtype (e.g., high‑grade serous vs clear cell vs low‑grade serous), as biology and chemosensitivity differ, and some subtypes may benefit from tailored systemic or hormonal approaches [@guideline:nccn_ovariancancer_v3_2025__v00000000|15; @pubmed:27558151].  
- Disparities in access to biomarker testing and PARP inhibitors significantly impact outcomes; MDTs should ensure timely testing, genetic counseling, and equitable access to targeted therapies when indicated [@pubmed:36643655].
